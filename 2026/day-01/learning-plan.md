# Day 01: My 90-Day DevOps Learning Plan

## My Understanding of DevOps & Cloud Engineering
DevOps is not just a set of tools; it is a culture of collaboration, automation, and continuous improvement between development and operations. Cloud Engineering is the backbone that supports this, providing the scalable infrastructure needed to build and deploy applications reliably. 

## Why I am Starting and Where I Want to Reach
As a **working professional**, My goal is to transition into a role where I can automate manual processes, build robust infrastructure, and confidently manage production environments. By the end of these 90 days, I want to be a self-sufficient DevOps engineer who can independently troubleshoot and architect cloud-native solutions.

## How I Will Stay Consistent
Balancing work and learning is tough, so I am relying on discipline rather than motivation. I will stick to a strict daily schedule, focus heavily on hands-on labs instead of just watching videos, and document my journey here to hold myself accountable.

## 3 Clear Goals for the Next 90 Days
1. **Deploy a production-grade application on Kubernetes** (Scaling, load balancing, and persistent storage).
2. **Automate infrastructure provisioning** from scratch using Infrastructure as Code (Terraform).
3. **Build an end-to-end CI/CD pipeline** that automatically tests and deploys code.

## 3 Core DevOps Skills I want to Build
1. **Linux Troubleshooting:** Mastering the command line, understanding system processes, and debugging issues.
2. **CI/CD Pipelines:** Writing clean, efficient pipelines (e.g., Jenkins or GitHub Actions).
3. **Kubernetes Debugging:** Learning how to effectively read cluster logs, fix crash loops, and ensure high reliability.

## Weekly Time Budget
To make steady progress without burning out, I am committing to the following schedule:
* **Weekdays:** 1 to 2 hours per day (Focusing on reading, quick hands-on, and daily tasks).
* **Weekends:** 5 to 6 hours total (Dedicated to live learning and review).
